My Advice, and Stereotype Threat

The last topic someone asked for my advice on was strategy for drafting a set of Magic: the Gathering with friends. I feel this somewhat reflects some of my values such as enjoying a challenge, being creative, and my search for effectiveness and efficiency. I personally get excited when faced with an exciting or interesting challenge, but I recognize that not everyone has the same values as me and may not enjoy the game in the same way I do so I try to take a neutral stance as I explain the various options the game has.
I think to be able to recognize others' values can help you mediate stereotype threat by helping realize why you might feel a certain way toward someone, be aware of how you wish to react to that, and how you wish to then mindfully interact with them.

The Happiest Little Values

If for whatever reason I'm feeling down at DBC, I think I'll try to focus on a value of mine that would make me happy to focus on and try to achieve something supporting that value. Learning even a tiny bit toward starting a new skill or hobby can be very rewarding.
